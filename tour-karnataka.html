Karnataka
Sacred Trail

The Karnataka coast is where the Western Ghats meet the Arabian Sea — the Karavali coast with its ancient temple towns, unique Tulu culture, and a tradition of temple management and ritual that differs markedly from both Tamil Nadu and Kerala. Udupi's Krishna tradition, Murudeshwar's sea-facing Shiva, Dharmasthala's composite sacred site — this is a less-visited, genuinely distinctive circuit.

The Journey

The Krishna of Udupi. The Shiva who faces the Arabian Sea.

Udupi is the birthplace of Madhvacharya — the 13th-century philosopher who founded Dvaita Vedanta, the tradition that understands the relationship between the individual soul and God as one of difference rather than unity. The Krishna temple he established here is one of the most distinctive in India: the deity is seen through a window of nine holes (Kanakana Kindi) rather than directly, in memory of the devotee Kanakadasa who was denied entry to the temple and then received darshan through a crack in the wall.

Murudeshwar, on a cape overlooking the Arabian Sea, has one of the largest Shiva statues in the world — 123 feet of white gopuram-topped stone visible from the sea. The temple itself is ancient; the statue is modern and spectacular. The combination of old and new, the sea, the ghats, and the energy of the site make it unforgettable.

Dharmasthala is a rarity — a temple administered by a Jain family (the Heggade family) but dedicated to Shiva and welcoming to all. It runs one of the largest free meal programs in India, feeding 20,000+ pilgrims daily from its own resources. The administration is a study in what sacred generosity can look like at scale.

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Mangalore · Kukke Subramanya

The serpent god

Arrive Mangalore airport. Drive to Kukke Subramanya (75km, 2 hours through the Ghats). Kukke Subramanya — the primary Subramanya (Murugan/Kartikeya) temple in Karnataka, set in a forested valley at the foot of the Kumara Parvata range. Known especially for Sarpa Dosha remedies — rituals for those with snake-related astrological afflictions. Overnight at the temple town.

  • Kukke Subramanya darshan

  • Dinner
02
Day 02

Dharmasthala · The Jain-Hindu Sacred Site

Generosity as religion

Drive to Dharmasthala (65km). Dharmasthala Manjunatha Temple — Shiva temple administered by the Jain Heggade family for over 800 years, feeding 20,000+ pilgrims daily for free, managing everything from lodging to charitable hospitals. The spirit of the place is extraordinary — a functioning model of what a sacred site could be when the administration puts service above commerce. Visit the Car Museum (the Heggade family's remarkable collection) and the Manjusha Museum.

Included Today
  • Dharmasthala Temple darshan
  • Free meal at the prasadam hall
  • Manjusha Museum
  • Car Museum
  • Hotel overnight
  • All meals
03
Day 03

Udupi · Krishna Darshan · The Nine-Hole Window

Madhvacharya's temple

Drive to Udupi (85km). Udupi Krishna Temple — darshan through the Kanakana Kindi (nine-hole window through which Kanakadasa received his vision). Morning puja with bells, lamps, and chanting in the Dvaita Vedanta tradition. The eight mathas surrounding the temple, each managed by a different Madhva Brahmana monastery in rotation. Malpe beach and St. Mary's Island (basalt formations).

Included Today
  • Udupi Krishna Temple morning darshan
  • Eight mathas visit
  • Malpe beach
  • Heritage hotel Udupi
  • All meals
04
Day 04

Murudeshwar · Gokarna

The sea-facing Shiva

Drive to Murudeshwar (60km). Murudeshwar temple and the 123-foot Shiva statue on the cape — one of the most dramatic temple locations in India, with the Arabian Sea on three sides. Underwater world (aquarium) and rope-way for aerial views. Continue to Gokarna (50km) — the Om Beach town with the Mahabaleshwar Temple, a Shiva shrine of considerable antiquity set in a traditional Karnataka coastal town largely unchanged by tourism.

Included Today
  • Murudeshwar temple and statue
  • Rope-way
  • Gokarna Mahabaleshwar Temple
  • Gokarna beach hotel
  • All meals
05
Day 05

Hampi · Virupaksha Temple

The Vijayanagara sacred city

Drive or fly to Hampi (4 hours or short flight from Hubli). Hampi — the ruins of the Vijayanagara empire, and simultaneously the landscape of Kishkindha from the Ramayana. Virupaksha Temple — the only continuously functioning temple in Hampi, with Lakshmi the elephant. Vittala Temple with the stone chariot and musical pillars. Sunset from Matanga Hill.

Included Today
  • Drive or fly to Hampi
  • Virupaksha Temple
  • Vittala Temple
  • Stone chariot
  • Hampi heritage hotel
  • All meals

Day 06

The final morning

Morning walk through the Hampi boulderscape at first light — the most beautiful hour in Hampi, when the rocks catch the low sun and the river is quiet. Transfer to Hubli airport or drive to Bangalore for your return.
