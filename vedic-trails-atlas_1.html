A Journey Through Sacred India

Five threads. One civilization.

The routes that have shaped Indian spiritual life for millennia.

01

Char Dham — the four shrines

In the 8th century, Adi Shankaracharya marked four temples at the four corners of India — a sacred geometry that has guided pilgrims for 1,200 years. Walk all four, and tradition says you wash away the karma of a lifetime.

View the Char Dham journey →
02

The Twelve Jyotirlingas

Twelve sites where Shiva is said to have manifested as a column of cosmic light. Spread across nine states from the Himalayas to the southern coast, they form one of the most precise sacred geometries in the world.

View the Jyotirlinga circuit →
03

Krishna's path through Braj

From his birthplace in Mathura's prison to the playful pastures of Vrindavan to the kingdom he ruled at Dwarka — the life of Krishna is mapped across India. Walking this trail is walking a story 5,000 years old.

View the Krishna trail →
04

South India's living temples

The greatest concentration of unbroken temple traditions on earth. The Chola, Pallava, and Pandya kings built temples that still function exactly as they did a thousand years ago — towering gopurams, thousand-pillared halls, daily rituals unchanged.

View the South India circuit →
05

Kashi — where time stops

The oldest continuously inhabited city in the world. Standing on the ghats of the Ganga at four in the morning, watching the first lamp meet the river — this is the moment where Indian spirituality stops being an idea and becomes something you have actually touched.

View the Kashi journey →
