Sacred Calendar

When to go,
where to go, and why

The sacred calendar of India is ancient, complex, and specific. Certain places are only fully alive at certain times. This is what we know about when to go where.

All dates follow the 2026 Hindu lunar calendar
शी
Winter Peak
Oct – Feb

The finest season for most circuits. Cool, dry, clear skies. Every major festival falls in this window. Char Dham, South India, Rajasthan all peak here.

वस
Spring
Mar – May

Holi in Braj (March). Char Dham opens (April/May). The Himalayas wake. Excellent for Mathura-Vrindavan, Vaishno Devi, and North India before the heat arrives.

वर्ष
Monsoon
Jun – Sep

Challenging for most circuits. Char Dham closes. However: South India temples are spectacular in monsoon, Kamakhya's Ambubachi Mela is June, and Jagannath Rath Yatra (Puri) is June-July.

शर
Autumn
Sep – Oct

Navratri (Oct) is among the most important festival periods. Dussehra, Diwali follow. Char Dham closes (Oct/Nov) for winter. The Himalayan pilgrimages make their final runs of the season.

Holi · Lathmar Holi
The original Holi is in Barsana and Nandgaon in Braj — the Lathmar Holi where women beat men with sticks. This is not the Holi of water balloons and gulal. It is the recreation of Krishna and Radha's play.

Annual festival of ritual transgression at Kodungallur Bhagavathy — one of the most unusual festivals in India. Kerala.

The birth anniversary of Rama. Ayodhya's Ram Mandir receives its largest footfall of the year. Rameswaram and Hampi are also significant on this day.

Buddha Purnima
The birth, enlightenment, and death anniversary of the Buddha — all said to occur on the same full moon. Bodh Gaya and Sarnath receive pilgrims from every Buddhist country. The Mahabodhi Temple at night on Buddha Purnima is extraordinary.

Ambubachi Mela · Kamakhya
The most unusual festival in India — the annual menstruation of the goddess Kamakhya. The temple closes for three days. Tens of thousands of Tantric practitioners, ascetics, and sadhus converge at Guwahati. Not for everyone. For those ready for it: irreplaceable.

The chariot procession of Jagannath — the largest chariot procession in the world. The deity leaves his temple and travels through the city in a chariot pulled by thousands of devotees. One million people attend.

Nine nights of the goddess. The most important festival period in the Shakta tradition. Kamakhya, Vaishno Devi, Vindhyavasini, and all 51 Shakti Peethas are at their most intense. Garba in Gujarat. Durga Puja in Bengal (Oct).

The most spectacular festival in Kolkata — five days of elaborately constructed pandals, goddess imagery, and collective celebration that transforms the city. The largest festival in Eastern India by participation.

The Himalayan shrines close for winter on auspicious dates in October-November. The closing ceremonies — particularly at Kedarnath — are extraordinarily atmospheric. Book by September.

Varanasi and Ayodhya are the most extraordinary places to spend Diwali — the illumination of the ghats, the fireworks over the Ganges, the scale of the celebration in the city where Rama returned from exile. Book far in advance.

Varanasi's own festival — on Kartik Purnima (the full moon after Diwali), 84 ghats along the Ganges are illuminated with one million lamps simultaneously. Witnessed from a boat on the river. One of the most visually overwhelming experiences in India.

Kerala's most spectacular festival — decorated elephants, percussion ensembles, and fireworks at the Vadakkunnathan Temple in Thrissur. The finest display of caparisoned elephants in the world.

Most auspicious dates for pilgrimage

Certain dates in the Hindu calendar carry particular weight for beginning a yatra. These are the dates our concierges most commonly recommend as departure points.

Ekadashi · Twice monthly
The Eleventh Day

The eleventh day of each lunar fortnight is sacred to Vishnu. Beginning a Vaishnava pilgrimage — Tirupati, Mathura, Char Dham, Dwarka — on Ekadashi is considered especially auspicious. Fasting is traditional on this day.

Akshaya Tritiya · Apr/May
The Undying Third

The third day of the bright fortnight of Vaishakha. Considered auspicious for beginning any important undertaking. The Char Dham temples traditionally open on this date. Anything begun today is said to grow without diminishing.

Kartik Purnima · Oct/Nov
The Full Moon of Kartik

The full moon of the month of Kartik — considered one of the most sacred days in the Hindu calendar. Dev Deepawali in Varanasi. Sacred bathing at Pushkar Lake. Guru Nanak Jayanti. Pilgrimage on this day is said to carry the merit of many ordinary pilgrimages.

Makar Sankranti · Jan 14
The Solar Transition

The sun enters Capricorn — a solar event, unlike most Hindu festivals which follow the lunar calendar. Sacred bathing in rivers is universally practiced. Prayagraj, Varanasi, Haridwar, and Rameswaram all receive massive pilgrimages on this date.

Maha Shivaratri · Feb
The Great Night of Shiva

The fourteenth night of the dark fortnight of Phalguna. The most significant night in the Shaiva calendar. All 12 Jyotirlinga temples perform special abhisheka through the night. Night vigil and fasting. Beginning a Jyotirlinga circuit on this date carries enormous merit.

Any Monday · Throughout year
Shiva's Day

Monday is Shiva's day. Beginning any Shiva-related pilgrimage — Jyotirlingas, Ujjain, Kashi — on a Monday is considered appropriate. The Mahakaleshwar Bhasma Aarti in Ujjain is most attended on Mondays.
