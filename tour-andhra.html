Sacred Trail

Andhra Pradesh contains two of India's most significant sacred sites — Tirupati, the most visited religious site on earth, and Srisailam, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas in a forested gorge on the Krishna river. It also contains Lepakshi — the hanging pillar temple — and the ruins of Amaravathi, the ancient Buddhist city.

The Journey

The most visited sacred site on earth. And the temple with the pillar that floats.

Tirupati receives 50,000-100,000 pilgrims every single day. No sacred site on earth — not the Vatican, not Mecca, not Jerusalem — receives more visitors annually. The Venkateswara temple on Tirumala hill has been continuously worshipped for at least 1,300 years, and the tradition it serves is considerably older. The scale of devotion here is staggering.

Four hours west, deep in the Nallamala forest on a gorge above the Krishna river, sits Srisailam — the Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga, one of the twelve sacred sites where Shiva manifested as a column of light. This is also a Shakti Peetha — one of the rare sites that is simultaneously a Jyotirlinga and a Shakti Peetha. The forest setting is extraordinary: no development for miles, the river below, the hills above.

Lepakshi, between Tirupati and Bangalore, contains the Veerabhadra Temple — 70 stone pillars from the 16th century, one of which does not appear to touch the floor. Five hundred years of visitors have been passing cloth beneath it. No satisfactory explanation has been offered.

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Tirupati · Ascend to Tirumala

The seven hills

Arrive Tirupati airport. Drive up the Seven Hills to Tirumala (26km of hairpin bends). Check into Tirumala guesthouse. Evening visit to secondary shrines — Varahaswami Temple (the guardian deity, older than Venkateswara himself). Register for the next morning's special darshan. Prasadam dinner.

Included Today
  • Tirupati airport pickup
  • Tirumala guesthouse
  • Varahaswami Temple
  • Prasadam dinner
02
Day 02

Tirupati · Suprabhatam · VIP Darshan

The wealthiest temple on earth

3:15 AM wakeup. Suprabhatam — the pre-dawn waking ceremony. Special Entry Darshan (₹300 category) — entry through the priority line, direct to the sanctum. Afternoon at Tirumala: Srivari Padalu (sacred footprints), temple museum. Descend to Tirupati town. Visit Govindaraja Swamy Temple — the great Vishnu temple in the town itself, far less visited than Tirumala.

Included Today
  • Suprabhatam attendance
  • Special Entry VIP darshan
  • Srivari Padalu
  • Govindaraja Swamy Temple
  • All meals
03
Day 03

Lepakshi · The Hanging Pillar

500 years, still floating

Drive to Lepakshi (135km from Tirupati). Veerabhadra Temple — the 16th-century Vijayanagara temple with 70 pillars and one that doesn't appear to touch the floor. The ceiling frescoes are the finest surviving Vijayanagara paintings in existence. The monolithic Nandi bull outside is the largest in Andhra Pradesh. The site of Jatayu's fall in the Ramayana. Continue drive toward Srisailam.

Included Today
  • Drive to Lepakshi
  • Veerabhadra Temple with fresco guide
  • Monolithic Nandi
  • Drive toward Srisailam
  • Forest rest house overnight
04
Day 04

Srisailam · Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga

The forest Jyotirlinga

Morning darshan at Mallikarjuna-Bhramaramba temple — the Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peetha combined, deep in the Nallamala forest. The forest setting, the Krishna river gorge below, the complete absence of development — makes this one of the most atmospherically extraordinary sacred sites in South India. Boat on the Nagarjuna Sagar reservoir. Drive back toward Hyderabad for the night.

Included Today
  • Mallikarjuna Jyotirlinga VIP darshan
  • Forest walk
  • Krishna river gorge viewpoint
  • Drive to Hyderabad
  • All meals

Day 05

Hyderabad · Departure

The final morning

Optional visit to the Birla Mandir — the white marble Venkateswara temple on a hill in Hyderabad, with panoramic views of the city. Transfer to Hyderabad airport for your return.
