The Journey

The oldest Vishnu temple. The most secretive treasury on earth.

Kerala's temple tradition is architecturally and ritually distinct from every other Indian state. The temples here do not have towering gopurams. They are low, dark, built in Kerala laterite and timber, roofed in copper or clay tiles, their sanctums lit by oil lamps rather than electricity. The architecture is called Kerala or Thachu Shastra — a tradition of sacred carpentry that goes back fifteen centuries and whose masters are still working today.

Guruvayur, dedicated to Krishna, is called the Dwarka of the South — the most important Vaishnava pilgrimage site in Kerala, where 80,000 people come daily and where the resident elephant herd has its own mahouts, veterinarians, and retirement protocols. Padmanabhaswamy in Thiruvananthapuram is the wealthiest temple in the world — its underground vaults, when partially opened in 2011, were found to contain gold, diamonds, and artefacts valued at over ₹1 lakh crore, the bulk of which remains unopened. Sabarimala on its hill in the Western Ghats receives the largest annual pilgrimage gathering in the world — 40-50 million people in a single season.

This circuit also includes Thrissur — the cultural capital of Kerala — during or near Thrissur Pooram, the festival that is simply the most spectacular display of decorated elephants, percussion, and fireworks in India.

"Padmanabhaswamy's Vault B has not been fully opened. The reasons given vary. The contents of what has been found suggest that what remains may be extraordinary."

The Itinerary

Day 01

Arrive Kochi · Ernakulam Shiva Temple

Into Kerala

Arrive Kochi airport. Drive to Ernakulam Shiva Temple — one of the oldest temples in Kochi, rarely visited by tourists, extremely atmospheric. Check into heritage hotel in Fort Kochi. Evening walk through the Dutch and Portuguese quarters. Dinner at a traditional Kerala thali restaurant.

Included Today
  • Kochi airport pickup
  • Ernakulam Shiva Temple visit
  • Fort Kochi heritage hotel
  • Kerala thali dinner
02
Day 02

Guruvayur · Krishna Darshan

The Dwarka of the South

Pre-dawn drive to Guruvayur (2.5 hours). Nirmalyam darshan at 3 AM — the opening ritual when the temple's garlands from the previous day are removed and the deity is seen in his natural state, considered the most auspicious darshan of the day. Full VIP darshan. Visit the Punnathur Kotta elephant sanctuary — the temple's own 60-elephant herd, the largest captive elephant population in the world. Afternoon: Mammiyur Mahadeva Temple (Shiva, adjacent to Guruvayur). Return to Thrissur for the night.

Included Today
  • Nirmalyam 3AM darshan
  • VIP darshan
  • Punnathur Kotta elephant sanctuary
  • Mammiyur Mahadeva Temple
  • Thrissur hotel
03
Day 03

Thrissur · Vadakkunnathan Temple

The heart of Kerala

Morning visit to Vadakkunnathan Temple — the oldest Shiva temple in Kerala, a UNESCO-listed monument, built entirely in the Kerala style with no stone carvings visible from outside. The murals inside depicting scenes from the Mahabharata are among the finest in India. Afternoon: Thrissur Pooram Museum (if not visiting during the actual festival) or preparations if timing aligns. Evening cultural programme — Kathakali or Mohiniyattam at the Kerala Kalamandalam.

Included Today
  • Vadakkunnathan Temple VIP darshan
  • Mural viewing
  • Cultural performance
  • All meals
04
Day 04

Sabarimala · Lord Ayyappa

The hill pilgrimage

Early drive to Pamba base camp (5 hours). Trek to Sabarimala temple (5km uphill through forest). Darshan at the Ayyappa shrine — a pilgrimage accessible to all regardless of caste, where the only dress code is black or blue (the colours of austerity). The 18 sacred steps leading to the shrine are each said to represent a spiritual state. Return to Pamba by evening. Note: Sabarimala is accessible to men and post-menopausal women only during the main season. We will advise on timing and access.

Included Today
  • Drive to Pamba
  • Forest trek
  • Sabarimala darshan
  • Return transport
  • All meals
05
Day 05

Thiruvananthapuram · Padmanabhaswamy Temple

The wealthiest temple on earth

Drive south to Thiruvananthapuram (3 hours). Padmanabhaswamy Temple — dedicated to Vishnu reclining on the serpent Ananta, one of the 108 Divya Desams of Vaishnavism and the most significant temple in Kerala. Non-Hindus are not admitted; Hindus can have darshan after completing a simple registration process that we will facilitate. The deity visible through three doors — head, navel, and feet — is said to be 18 feet long. Visit to Kuthiramalika Palace Museum for Kerala royal artefacts.

Included Today
  • Drive to Thiruvananthapuram
  • Padmanabhaswamy Temple (Hindu guests)
  • Kuthiramalika Palace Museum
  • Heritage hotel overnight
06
Day 06

Varkala · Janardhana Swamy Temple · Backwaters

The cliff temple

Morning visit to Janardhana Swamy Temple at Varkala — a 2000-year-old Vishnu temple on the cliff edge above the Arabian Sea. One of the most dramatically situated temples in India. Afternoon: backwater boat through Poovar or Ashtamudi lake — the Kerala waterway system that connects the inland lagoons to the sea. Sunset from the water.

Included Today
  • Janardhana Swamy Temple
  • Backwater boat ride
  • Seafood dinner at cliff restaurant
  • Varkala beach hotel
07
Day 07

Departure · Trivandrum

The final morning

Early morning optional visit to Attukal Bhagavathy Temple — the temple that holds the Guinness World Record for the largest gathering of women for a religious ritual (the Pongala festival draws over 3 million women). Transfer to Trivandrum airport for your return.

Included Today
  • Attukal Bhagavathy Temple (optional)
  • Airport transfer
  • Prasadam for your journey
