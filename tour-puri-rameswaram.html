The Journey

Three corners of sacred India. The fourth is already in you.

Adi Shankaracharya, the 8th-century philosopher and reformer, established four monasteries (mathas) at the four geographic extremities of India and designated the four temples associated with them as the Char Dham — the four abodes. Badrinath in the north (Himalayas), Puri in the east (Bay of Bengal), Rameswaram in the south (Indian Ocean), Dwarka in the west (Arabian Sea). Completing all four is considered one of the most significant pilgrimages in Hinduism.

This circuit visits three — Puri, Rameswaram, and Dwarka — the three coastal Char Dhams that can be combined in one arc. Badrinath in the Himalayas is covered in our Char Dham Yatra. Together they complete the full set.

Each of the three is extraordinary in its own right. Puri has the Jagannath Temple — where the deity is not carved stone but sacred wood, replaced every twelve years in a ceremony whose inner rituals are known only to the hereditary priests. Rameswaram has the longest corridor of any temple in India. Dwarka, according to the Mahabharata, has an earlier version of itself lying under the Arabian Sea.

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Bhubaneswar · Puri

The eastern Char Dham

Arrive Bhubaneswar airport. Drive to Puri (60km). Puri — the Jagannath Temple, one of the four Char Dhams, dedicated to Jagannath (Lord of the Universe — a form of Vishnu/Krishna). The deity here is made of sacred neem wood and is replaced every 12 years in the Nabakalebara ceremony. Non-Hindus are not admitted; we will arrange viewing from the Raghunandan Library rooftop, the traditional vantage point. Evening at the beach — the Bay of Bengal at sunset.

Included Today
  • Bhubaneswar airport pickup
  • Drive to Puri
  • Jagannath Temple (Hindu darshan or library viewing)
  • Puri beach hotel
  • Welcome dinner
02
Day 02

Puri · Konark · Chilika Lake

The Sun Temple and the bird lake

Konark Sun Temple (UNESCO) — the 13th-century temple built as a giant chariot for the Sun God, with 24 elaborately carved wheels serving as sundials. One of the most spectacular pieces of architecture in India, even in its ruined state. Afternoon: Chilika Lake — Asia's largest brackish lagoon, a migratory bird sanctuary and the site of the Maa Kalijai Temple on an island in the middle of the lake. Boat to the island.

Included Today
  • Konark Sun Temple
  • Chilika Lake boat
  • Maa Kalijai island temple
  • All meals
03
Day 03

Fly to Madurai · Drive to Rameswaram

The southern Char Dham

Fly Bhubaneswar to Madurai. Drive to Rameswaram (4 hours). Rameswaram — the Ramanathaswamy Temple, Jyotirlinga and Char Dham, with the longest corridor of any temple in India (1,212 metres). 22 sacred wells within the temple, each with different water chemistry and spiritual attribution. Evening arrival, Pamban Bridge crossing.

Included Today
  • Flight to Madurai
  • Drive to Rameswaram via Pamban Bridge
  • Rameswaram hotel
  • First darshan
04
Day 04

Rameswaram · The 22 Wells · Dhanushkodi

The edge of India

Pre-dawn Agni Theertham ritual bath at the beach. Full darshan at Ramanathaswamy Temple — 22 sacred well ritual bath sequence, each well blessed by a different deity. Drive to Dhanushkodi — the ghost town at the tip of Rameswaram island. Adam's Bridge (Ram Setu) visible from the coast. Gandamadana Parvatham temple on the highest hill of Rameswaram.

Included Today
  • Agni Theertham ritual
  • Ramanathaswamy VIP darshan
  • 22 sacred wells sequence
  • Dhanushkodi and Adam's Bridge viewpoint
  • All meals
05
Day 05

Fly to Ahmedabad · Drive to Dwarka

The western Char Dham

Fly Madurai to Ahmedabad. Drive to Dwarka (5 hours or overnight train). Dwarka — the Dwarkadhish Temple, Char Dham, built on the site of Krishna's kingdom. The temple spire (78 metres) dominates the skyline. Evening darshan. The flag on the spire is changed five times daily by the hereditary flag-bearer family.

Included Today
  • Flight to Ahmedabad
  • Drive or train to Dwarka
  • Dwarkadhish Temple evening darshan
  • Sea-facing hotel
06
Day 06

Dwarka · Bet Dwarka · Nageshwar

The island and the Jyotirlinga

Pre-dawn Mangala aarti at Dwarkadhish. Boat to Bet Dwarka — the island where Krishna actually lived, separate from the administrative city. Nageshwar Jyotirlinga (15km from Dwarka) — one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, with the famous large outdoor Shiva murti. Return to Dwarka.

Included Today
  • Pre-dawn Dwarkadhish aarti
  • Bet Dwarka island boat
  • Nageshwar Jyotirlinga
  • All meals
07
Day 07

Somnath · The First Jyotirlinga

Rebuilt seven times

Drive south to Somnath (5 hours along the Saurashtra coast). Somnath — the first Jyotirlinga, destroyed by invaders at least six times and rebuilt each time. The current temple, built in 1951, stands at the exact confluence of the Arabian Sea, Hiran river, and underground Saraswati. Evening temple aarti facing the sea.

Included Today
  • Coastal drive
  • Somnath Jyotirlinga darshan
  • Triveni Sangam
  • Somnath hotel
  • All meals
08
Day 08

Departure · Diu

The final morning

Morning visit to Bhalka Tirtha — the site where Krishna was struck by the hunter's arrow, 2km from Somnath. Transfer to Diu airport (90km) for your return.

Included Today
  • Bhalka Tirtha
  • Drive to Diu airport
  • Prasadam for your journey
