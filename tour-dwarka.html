The Journey

A kingdom under the sea. A temple rebuilt seven times.

Dwarka is where Krishna came after leaving Mathura and Vrindavan. He built a city here on the Saurashtra coast, ruled it as a king, and according to the Mahabharata, the city submerged into the sea after he died. Marine archaeologists have found structures 30-40 meters below the surface of the Gulf of Khambhat that some scholars connect to this account. The temple standing on the shore today — the Dwarkadhish temple, with its 78-meter spire — is one of the four Char Dham and one of the holiest sites for Vaishnavas in India.

Somnath, five hours south along the coast, is the first of the twelve Jyotirlingas. It has been destroyed by invaders at least six times — by Al-Qāhir, Mahmud of Ghazni, Alauddin Khalji, Muzaffar Shah, and Aurangzeb — and rebuilt each time. The current temple, built in 1951 in the Chalukya style at the initiative of Sardar Patel, is the seventh. Inside is the same tradition of Shiva worship that has been maintained, interrupted and restarted, for more than fifteen centuries. The temple stands literally at the point where the Arabian Sea, the Lune River, and the Saraswati (underground) meet — at coordinates the ancient astronomers identified as the "Prabha Tirtha."

Five days along this coast — Dwarka, Bet Dwarka, Nageshwar, Somnath — take you through some of the most geographically and historically layered sacred terrain in India.

"The first time Somnath was destroyed, the priests simply moved the sacred flame to a safe place and began planning the next temple. It took six invasions before anyone thought to stop rebuilding."

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Jamnagar · Drive to Dwarka

To the western Char Dham

Fly to Jamnagar (nearest airport to Dwarka). Drive along the Saurashtra coast to Dwarka (2 hours). Afternoon arrival. Check into sea-facing hotel. Evening visit to the Rukmini Devi Temple, dedicated to Krishna's primary wife — a quieter, more intimate site than the main Dwarkadhish temple, rarely crowded. Sunset at the Gomti Ghat where the Gomti river meets the sea.

Included Today
  • Jamnagar airport pickup
  • Coastal drive to Dwarka
  • Sea-facing heritage hotel
  • Rukmini Devi Temple and Gomti Ghat
  • Welcome dinner with Gujarati thali
02
Day 02

Dwarkadhish · Bet Dwarka

The kingdom and the island

Pre-dawn Mangala aarti at the Dwarkadhish Temple, then full VIP darshan. The temple's morning ritual involves changing the flag on its spire — a 78-meter column of stone — five times a day, by a hereditary family of flag-bearers whose lineage stretches back centuries. Afternoon boat trip to Bet Dwarka — the island 30 minutes offshore where Krishna actually lived with his family, separate from the administrative city. The island has a timeless, unhurried quality that the mainland temple town lacks.

Included Today
  • Pre-dawn Dwarkadhish Mangala aarti
  • VIP darshan
  • Boat trip to Bet Dwarka
  • Island temple visits
  • Sunset dinner by the sea
03
Day 03

Nageshwar Jyotirlinga · Drive toward Somnath

The lord of serpents

Morning darshan at Nageshwar — one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, 15 km from Dwarka, famous for its enormous outdoor Shiva murti (the largest Shiva statue in India at the time it was built). Drive south along the Saurashtra coast to Porbandar — Gandhi's birthplace — for a brief visit to the Kirti Mandir (his family home, now a museum). Continue to Somnath for evening arrival. Sunset at the Somnath beach temple.

Included Today
  • Nageshwar Jyotirlinga darshan
  • Coastal drive
  • Porbandar Gandhi birthplace (optional)
  • Somnath hotel near temple
  • Evening at the sea
04
Day 04

Somnath · Bhalka Tirtha · Triveni Sangam

Where the rivers meet the sea

Pre-dawn Somnath darshan — the Jyotirlinga that faces directly west toward Mecca (unusual among temples), built so the prana-pratishtha line extends unbroken to Antarctica with no land in between. Visit to Bhalka Tirtha — 2 km from Somnath, the site where Krishna was struck by the hunter's arrow that ended his earthly life. Then to the Triveni Sangam — where the Arabian Sea, Hiranya River, and the underground Saraswati meet — considered among the holiest of confluences. Afternoon rest at Somnath.

Day 05

Gir Forest · Departure

The last Asiatic lions

Optional morning jeep safari at Gir National Park — the last wild habitat of the Asiatic lion, 65 km from Somnath (₹3,500 add-on per person, booking in advance essential). Otherwise, a reflective morning at the Somnath beach temple. Transfer to Diu airport (90 km, 1.5 hours) for your return flight.
