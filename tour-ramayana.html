Ramayana
Trail

The Ramayana is not mythology in the Western sense — it is sacred geography. Every major location in Valmiki's epic corresponds to a real place in India and Sri Lanka, each of which is a living pilgrimage site. This circuit traces the journey of Rama from his birthplace in Ayodhya to the sea at Rameswaram — the complete arc of the epic walked as a pilgrimage.

The Journey

From his birthplace in Ayodhya to the bridge at the edge of India.

Valmiki wrote the Ramayana approximately 2,500 years ago. The places he describes — Ayodhya, Chitrakoot, Panchavati, Lanka — have been identified, debated, and walked as pilgrimage routes for as long as the text has existed. The Archaeological Survey of India has found structures at Ayodhya that correspond to descriptions in the text. The Adam's Bridge between India and Sri Lanka — a chain of limestone shoals — has been identified with the Ram Setu described in the Ramayana since at least the 5th century CE.

This circuit begins at Ayodhya — Rama's birthplace, now transformed by the Ram Mandir consecrated in 2024, the most significant new temple in India in a generation. It moves through Chitrakoot (where Rama spent the first 11 years of his exile), Nashik (the Panchavati forest where Sita was abducted), and ends at Rameswaram — the southernmost point of the mainland, where Rama built the bridge to Lanka and where he worshipped Shiva for the sin of killing Ravana.

The circuit is a north-to-south traversal of India — from the Gangetic plains to the Tamil coast — following the exact direction of Rama's own journey. Done in this direction, it has a geographic logic that makes the myth feel like biography.

"Rama built the Ram Setu to Lanka. He then worshipped Shiva at Rameswaram for the sin of killing Ravana — a Brahmin, regardless of his crimes. The ethical complexity of this is not modern. It is in the original text."

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Lucknow · Drive to Ayodhya

The birthplace of Rama

Arrive Lucknow airport. Drive to Ayodhya (2 hours). Ayodhya has been transformed — the Ram Mandir, consecrated in January 2024 on the site of the Ram Janmabhoomi, is now the most significant new temple in India. Evening darshan at the Ram Mandir. Walk the Parikrama Marg — the circumambulation path around the city, 14km, representing the 14 years of Rama's exile. Dinner and overnight in Ayodhya.

  • Ram Mandir evening darshan
  • Ayodhya heritage hotel

02
Day 02

Ayodhya · The Five Mandals

The complete sacred city

Full day in Ayodhya. Morning darshan at the Ram Mandir at sunrise. Visit to Kanak Bhavan — the palace given to Sita by her mother-in-law Kaikeyi, now one of the most beautifully maintained temples in Ayodhya. Hanuman Garhi — the 10th-century Hanuman temple on a hill in the centre of the city. Dashrath Mahal. Saryu river ghat — sunset aarti on the river where Rama finally returned from exile. The ghats of Ayodhya have a quiet power distinct from Varanasi's intensity.

Included Today
  • Ram Mandir sunrise darshan
  • Kanak Bhavan
  • Hanuman Garhi
  • Saryu ghat aarti
  • All meals
03
Day 03

Chitrakoot · The Forest Exile

Eleven years in the hills

Drive to Chitrakoot (230km, 5 hours through the Vindhya hills). Chitrakoot — where Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana spent the first 11 years of the 14-year exile. Kamadgiri — the sacred hill that is circumambulated instead of climbed (circumambulation of the hill itself is the ritual). Ramghat on the Mandakini river — where Bharata met Rama and begged him to return. Sati Anusuiya Temple. Overnight in Chitrakoot.

Included Today
  • Drive to Chitrakoot
  • Kamadgiri Parikrama
  • Ramghat
  • Sati Anusuiya Temple
  • All meals
04
Day 04

Nashik · Panchavati · The Forest of the Abduction

Where Sita was taken

Fly or drive to Nashik. Panchavati — the forest grove named for five banyan trees where Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana built their forest home during the later years of exile. The Sita Gupha — the cave where Sita sheltered. The Tapovan — the meditation ground. The banks of the Godavari at Nashik where ritual bathing purifies. This is also where Shurpanakha encountered Rama — the event that set the abduction in motion.

Included Today
  • Flight or drive to Nashik
  • Panchavati complex
  • Sita Gupha
  • Godavari ghat ritual
  • Nashik hotel
  • All meals

Day 05

Hampi · Kishkindha · The Monkey Kingdom

Where Hanuman was born

Fly to Hubli, drive to Hampi (1 hour). Hampi — the ruins of Vijayanagara empire, and the landscape identified as Kishkindha, the monkey kingdom of Sugriva and Hanuman. Anjanadri Hill — the birthplace of Hanuman. The Pampa Sarovar. The Rishyamukha hills where Rama met Sugriva. Walking through the Hampi boulderscape at sunrise is one of the most extraordinary landscape experiences in India.

Included Today
  • Flight to Hubli + drive to Hampi
  • Anjanadri Hill Hanuman temple
  • Pampa Sarovar
  • Hampi boulderscape walk
  • Hampi heritage hotel
  • All meals
06
Day 06

Hampi · Virupaksha · Evening Light

The Vijayanagara temples

Full day in Hampi. Virupaksha Temple — the oldest functioning temple in Hampi, dedicated to Shiva, with an elephant named Lakshmi who blesses devotees at the entrance each morning. The Vittala Temple with its famous stone chariot and musical pillars (pillars that produce musical notes when tapped). Sunset from Matanga Hill — the highest point in Hampi, from which the entire landscape of the Tungabhadra river and boulder fields is visible.

Included Today
  • Virupaksha Temple with Lakshmi the elephant
  • Vittala Temple musical pillars
  • Matanga Hill sunset
  • All meals
07
Day 07

Rameswaram · The Bridge to Lanka

The southernmost point

Fly to Madurai, drive to Rameswaram (4 hours). Rameswaram — one of the four Char Dhams and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the southernmost temple in the mainland pilgrimage circuit. Rama built the Ram Setu from here to Lanka. After killing Ravana, he returned here to worship Shiva for the sin of killing a Brahmin. The Ramanathaswamy Temple has the longest corridor of any temple in India — 1,212 metres of carved stone pillars. 22 sacred wells within the temple, each with different water.

Included Today
  • Fly to Madurai + drive to Rameswaram
  • Ramanathaswamy Temple VIP darshan
  • 22 sacred wells ritual bath
  • Pamban Bridge crossing
  • Rameswaram hotel
08
Day 08

Rameswaram · Dhanushkodi · Adam's Bridge

The edge of India

Morning: Agni Theertham — the sacred beach where pilgrims take a ritual bath in the sea before entering the Ramanathaswamy Temple. Drive to Dhanushkodi — the ghost town at the tip of Rameswaram island, destroyed by the 1964 cyclone and never rebuilt. From here the chain of islands and shoals of Adam's Bridge (Ram Setu) is visible stretching toward Sri Lanka. This is the most geographically dramatic point of the entire circuit.

Included Today
  • Agni Theertham beach ritual
  • Dhanushkodi ghost town
  • Adam's Bridge viewpoint
  • Gandamadana Parvatham temple
  • All meals

Day 09

The journey complete

Optional early morning final darshan at Ramanathaswamy Temple. Drive to Madurai airport or Rameswaram station for your return. The circuit from Ayodhya in the north to Rameswaram in the south mirrors Rama's own journey — one of the longest sacred geographies in the world, completed.
