Rajasthan
Sacred Trail

Rajasthan is known for its forts and palaces. Its sacred geography is less visited and more unusual — the only Brahma temple in the world, a Krishna temple that has been continuously worshipped since the 14th century, Jain temples carved from marble over 1,500 years, and the Nathdwara shrine that is the spiritual heart of the Pushti Marg tradition.

The Journey

The only Brahma temple on earth. The most elaborate Jain marble carving in existence.

There is only one temple in the world primarily dedicated to Brahma — the creator god of the Hindu Trimurti. It is in Pushkar, Rajasthan, on the edge of a sacred lake that is said to have been created when Brahma dropped a lotus flower from the sky. The lake has 52 ghats. Pilgrims bathe here to be freed from the cycle of birth and death. The town around it is small, ancient, and unlike anywhere else in India.

Nathdwara, two hours south, is the seat of the Shrinathji temple — a manifestation of Krishna as a seven-year-old child, worshipped through the Pushti Marg tradition founded by Vallabhacharya in the 15th century. The temple is decorated eight times daily in a different style — Shringar, the decoration of the deity, is a high art form here, and the paintings that record these decorations (Pichwai paintings) are among the most distinctive art forms in Rajasthan.

Ranakpur, in the Aravalli hills, contains the Chaturmukha Dharana Vihara — a 15th-century Jain temple with 1,444 intricately carved marble pillars, no two of which are identical. It is one of the five most sacred Jain pilgrimage sites in India and arguably the most architecturally extraordinary.

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Jaipur · Govind Dev Ji Temple

The pink city's divine heart

Arrive Jaipur airport. Visit Govind Dev Ji Temple — the primary Krishna temple of Jaipur, located within the City Palace complex. Founded by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II who brought the Govind Dev Ji deity from Vrindavan to protect it during Aurangzeb's temple destruction campaigns. Evening aarti at the temple. Check into heritage haveli hotel.

02
Day 02

Pushkar · The Brahma Temple · Sacred Lake

The only Brahma temple on earth

Drive to Pushkar (3 hours). The Brahma Temple — the only major temple in India dedicated primarily to Brahma, built in the 14th century with a distinctive red spire and the symbol of Brahma's vehicle, the hamsa (swan), on the inner walls. Ritual bath at Pushkar Lake — 52 ghats, the sacred water said to wash away a lifetime of karma. Walk the market and stay the night in Pushkar.

Included Today
  • Drive to Pushkar
  • Brahma Temple darshan
  • Pushkar Lake ghat ritual
  • Pushkar desert hotel

03
Day 03

Nathdwara · Shrinathji · Eklingji

The child Krishna and the clan god of Mewar

Drive to Nathdwara (2 hours). Shrinathji Temple — darshan of the 7-year-old Krishna, decorated in the morning Shringar style. The Pichwai paintings in and around the temple are extraordinary. Afternoon drive to Eklingji (30 minutes from Udaipur) — the personal deity of the Mewar royal family, a four-faced Shiva image in black marble, worshipped continuously by the Mewar kings since the 8th century. Check into Udaipur heritage hotel.

Included Today
  • Nathdwara Shrinathji darshan
  • Pichwai painting viewing
  • Eklingji Temple darshan
  • Udaipur palace hotel
  • All meals
04
Day 04

Ranakpur · The Marble Labyrinth

1,444 pillars, no two the same

Drive to Ranakpur (2 hours through the Aravalli hills). The Chaturmukha Dharana Vihara Jain temple — built between 1437 and 1458 CE, covering 48,000 square feet, with 1,444 marble pillars each carved with different patterns. The central dome creates a pattern of light that moves through the building over the course of the day. Afternoon visit to the adjacent Surya (Sun) and Amba Mata temples. Return to Udaipur.

Included Today
  • Drive to Ranakpur
  • Jain temple with specialist guide
  • Surya Temple
  • Return drive
  • All meals

Day 05

Udaipur · Jagdish Temple · Lake Pichola

The city of lakes

Morning visit to Jagdish Temple — the largest temple in Udaipur, a 17th-century Vishnu shrine built by Maharana Jagat Singh in the Indo-Aryan style, with a continuous rhythm of devotional music. Boat ride on Lake Pichola to Jag Mandir island — where the Mughal Prince Shah Jahan took refuge in 1623. Afternoon at leisure in Udaipur's old city.

Departure · Udaipur

The final morning

Morning visit to Saheliyon Ki Bari — the garden of the maidens, built for the royal ladies of Udaipur — and the Maharana Pratap memorial for context on Mewar's sacred history. Transfer to Udaipur airport for your return.
