The Journey

Twelve hundred years of pilgrimage.

Twelve centuries ago, Adi Shankaracharya looked at the map of Bharat and saw something no one before him had seen — a sacred geometry. He marked four shrines at the four corners of India and called them the Char Dham. To this day, every Hindu who completes the circuit is considered to have completed the highest pilgrimage of their lifetime.

The four shrines are not chosen by chance. Yamunotri is the source of the Yamuna. Gangotri is the source of the Ganga. Kedarnath, at 11,755 feet, is among the highest Shiva shrines anywhere in the world. And Badrinath, dedicated to Vishnu, sits between the Nar and Narayan mountains on the banks of the Alaknanda.

These eleven days will take you through high-altitude Himalayan valleys, across passes where the air thins and time slows. You will arrive at temples at four in the morning, when the first conch is blown. You will bathe in glacial waters that priests have called holy for a millennium.

"To complete the Char Dham is not to visit four temples. It is to trace the geometry of a civilization."

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrival in Haridwar

Where the Ganga enters the plains

Arrival in Dehradun or Haridwar. Drive to your heritage hotel by the Ganga. Evening attendance at the Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri — one of the most powerful religious experiences in India, with hundreds of lamps floating downstream as priests chant ancient mantras.

Included Today
  • Pickup from Dehradun airport or Haridwar station
  • Heritage hotel by the Ganga
  • Reserved seating for Ganga Aarti
  • Welcome dinner with sattvic Garhwali cuisine
02
Day 02

Haridwar to Barkot

Into the Garhwal hills

Drive through the Garhwal foothills via Mussoorie. Stop for tea at the Lal Tibba viewpoint, then descend into the Yamuna valley. Overnight at Barkot — the gateway to Yamunotri.

Included Today
  • Air-conditioned vehicle with experienced mountain driver
  • Stop at Mussoorie viewpoint
  • 3-star hotel at Barkot
  • All meals
03
Day 03

Yamunotri Darshan

The first shrine

Drive to Janki Chatti, then a guided pony ride or palanquin journey up to the Yamunotri temple at 3,293m. Hot springs at the temple — pilgrims traditionally cook rice in the boiling water and offer it to the goddess. Return to Barkot by evening.

Included Today
  • Pony or palanquin (your choice) for the temple ascent
  • Personal pilgrimage guide
  • Hot springs entry and rice-offering ritual
  • Priest-led puja
04
Day 04

Barkot to Uttarkashi

Through the temple town

Drive to Uttarkashi — known as the Kashi of the North. Visit the ancient Vishwanath Temple along the way. Overnight at Uttarkashi on the banks of the Bhagirathi (one of the source rivers of the Ganga).

Included Today
  • Mountain drive with photo stops
  • Vishwanath Temple darshan
  • 4-star riverside hotel
  • All meals
05
Day 05

Gangotri Darshan

Where the Ganga descends

Pre-dawn drive to Gangotri at 3,100m. The temple stands where the Ganga is said to have descended from Shiva's matted hair. Witness the morning aarti at the temple, dip in the freezing Bhagirathi, and offer prayers at Bhagirath Shila — the rock where King Bhagirath meditated for the goddess to come to earth.

Included Today
  • VIP darshan with priority queue
  • Priest-led abhishekam
  • Bhagirath Shila visit
  • Return drive to Uttarkashi
06
Day 06

Uttarkashi to Guptkashi

The long mountain road

A full day of driving through some of the most spectacular Himalayan scenery in India — past Tehri Dam, through Rudraprayag (the confluence of Alaknanda and Mandakini), and into Guptkashi. Evening to rest before the most demanding leg of the journey.

Included Today
  • Long mountain drive (10 hours with stops)
  • Lunch at Rudraprayag overlooking the rivers
  • Boutique hotel at Guptkashi
  • Early dinner
07
Day 07

Kedarnath — Helicopter or Trek

The throne of Shiva

Helicopter to Kedarnath (15 minutes, recommended for older pilgrims) or guided trek (16km, 7-8 hours). Darshan at the 1,200-year-old Kedarnath temple at 11,755 feet. Stand before the lingam said to have been worshipped by the Pandavas after the Mahabharata. Return helicopter or overnight stay in Kedarnath (your choice).

Included Today
  • Helicopter from Phata heliport (or guided trek with porter)
  • VIP darshan and abhishekam at Kedarnath
  • Hot meals at Kedarnath
  • All transfers
08
Day 08

Guptkashi to Badrinath

To the throne of Vishnu

Drive to Badrinath via Joshimath and the famous Hanuman Chatti viewpoint. Badrinath is the only Char Dham shrine dedicated to Vishnu, sitting between the Nar and Narayan mountains. Evening aarti at the temple, where ancient hymns from the Vedas are still chanted exactly as they were 3,000 years ago.

Included Today
  • Mountain drive with viewpoint stops
  • Joshimath visit (winter seat of Badrinath)
  • Premium hotel at Badrinath
  • Evening aarti darshan
09
Day 09

Badrinath Darshan & Mana Village

The last village in India

Pre-dawn VIP darshan at Badrinath, followed by abhishekam ceremony in your name. Afternoon visit to Mana — the last village before the Tibetan border, where the Saraswati river still flows (above ground), and where the Pandavas began their final journey to heaven according to the Mahabharata. Return to Badrinath for evening rest.

Included Today
  • VIP darshan with priority queue
  • Abhishekam in your family's name
  • Mana village guided visit
  • Vyas Gufa and Bhima Pul
10
Day 10

Badrinath to Rishikesh

The descent

Long drive back to Rishikesh — the world capital of yoga, on the banks of the Ganga. Evening at leisure. Optional sunset Ganga aarti at Triveni Ghat.

Included Today
  • Long mountain drive (8-9 hours)
  • Boutique riverside hotel at Rishikesh
  • Optional Ganga aarti
11
Day 11

Rishikesh to Departure

A final blessing

Morning at leisure — walk across the Lakshman Jhula or Ram Jhula suspension bridges, visit Parmarth Niketan, or sit by the river. Transfer to Dehradun airport or Haridwar station in time for your onward journey.

Included Today
  • Late checkout
  • Drop to airport or station
  • Pilgrimage completion certificate
