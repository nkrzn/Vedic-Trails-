The Journey

The goddess is present here. Not represented. Present.

The Shakti Peetha tradition begins with an act of grief so absolute it reshapes the world. Sati, daughter of Daksha, immolated herself after her father insulted her husband Shiva. Shiva, mad with grief, carried her corpse across the universe. Vishnu cut her body into 51 pieces with his Sudarshana Chakra to free Shiva from his madness. Each piece fell to earth. Each site became a Shakti Peetha — a place where the goddess is not merely worshipped but is physically present in the earth, in the rock, in the spring.

At Kamakhya, the yoni fell. At Kalighat, the toes of the right foot. At Jwala Ji, the tongue. At Vindhyavasini, the right hand. At each of these sites, there is no image in the ordinary sense. The site itself is the deity. The rock, the flame, the spring — these are Sati's body, still present after how many thousands of years no one knows.

This is not mythology. This is sacred geography — the belief that the earth itself is divine, and that in certain places the divine is concentrated, accessible, and responsive. The Shakti Peethas are the primary expression of this belief in Hinduism.

Day by Day

The Itinerary

01
Day 01

Arrive Kolkata · Kalighat Temple

The toes of Sati

Arrive Kolkata airport. Kalighat Temple — one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, where the toes of Sati's right foot fell. The current structure dates to 1809 but the site is ancient beyond documentation. The black stone image of Kali here is roughly carved, more raw than most temple deities, and the atmosphere is intense. The Adi Ganga (old course of the Ganges) flows beside it. Check into heritage hotel.

02
Day 02

Kolkata · Dakshineswar · Belur Math

Ramakrishna's world

Dakshineswar Kali Temple on the banks of the Ganges — where Ramakrishna Paramahamsa spent most of his life as priest, where his mystical experiences shaped modern Hinduism, and where the image of Kali (Bhavatarini — she who liberates the world) is said to have spoken to him. Belur Math across the river — the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Mission, designed by Vivekananda himself, where the architecture synthesises Hindu, Islamic, and Christian elements.

  • Dakshineswar Temple VIP darshan
  • Ramakrishna shrine visit
  • Belur Math
  • Ganga boat crossing
  • All meals
03
Day 03

Fly to Varanasi · Vindhyavasini Temple

The right hand of Sati

Fly Kolkata to Varanasi. Drive to Vindhyavasini (85km). Vindhyavasini Shakti Peetha at Vindyachal — where Sati's right hand fell, one of the most powerful Shakti Peethas in North India. The surrounding region has three temples forming a sacred triangle — Vindhyavasini, Ashtabhuja Devi, and Kali Khoh — circumambulated as the Parikrama. Return to Varanasi overnight.

Included Today
  • Flight Kolkata to Varanasi
  • Drive to Vindyachal
  • Vindhyavasini Temple darshan
  • Sacred triangle Parikrama
  • Varanasi hotel
04
Day 04

Varanasi · Vishalakshi · Ganga Aarti

Sati's eyes

Vishalakshi Temple in Varanasi — the Shakti Peetha where Sati's eyes (or earrings, in some traditions) fell. Located within the dense lanes of the old city near Manikarnika Ghat. Morning Ganga boat at dawn — the most beautiful hour in Varanasi, when the river catches the first light and the city begins its daily ritual. Evening Ganga aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat.

Included Today
  • Vishalakshi Temple
  • Dawn Ganga boat
  • Manikarnika Ghat walk
  • Dashashwamedh Ganga aarti
  • All meals
05
Day 05

Fly to Guwahati · Kamakhya Temple

The most powerful Shakti Peetha

Fly Varanasi to Guwahati. Kamakhya Temple on Nilachal Hill — where the yoni of Sati fell, considered the most powerful of all Shakti Peethas. The sanctum contains no image — the natural rock fissure and spring are the goddess. VIP darshan through our established temple relationships. The Ambubachi Mela (if timing allows) — the annual festival of the goddess's menstruation, when tens of thousands of Tantric practitioners converge. Overnight Guwahati.

Included Today
  • Flight to Guwahati
  • Kamakhya Temple VIP darshan
  • Nilachal Hill shrines
  • Guwahati heritage hotel
  • All meals
06
Day 06

Fly to Chandigarh · Jwala Ji Temple

The tongue of fire

Fly to Chandigarh, drive to Jwala Ji (3 hours). Jwala Ji Shakti Peetha in the Kangra valley — where Sati's tongue fell, manifested as nine eternal flames burning from fissures in the rock without fuel. No idol. The flame is the goddess. Akbar's gold umbrella that turned to unknown metal is still kept here. Drive to Shimla or Chandigarh overnight.

Included Today
  • Flight to Chandigarh
  • Drive to Jwala Ji
  • Jwala Ji darshan and flames
  • Return drive
  • Overnight hotel
