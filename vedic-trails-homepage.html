The Living Calendar

Time, the way India keeps it.

Some journeys are best taken on a specific day — a festival, a lunar phase, an alignment that has been observed for centuries. These are the moments worth waiting for.

12 days away

Kartik Purnima

Varanasi · The Ganga

A million earthen lamps set afloat on the Ganga at dusk. The ghats become a galaxy. The most beautiful night of the Hindu year.

Plan this journey →

38 days away

Geeta Jayanti

Kurukshetra · Brahma Sarovar

The day the Bhagavad Gita was spoken. The sacred pond glows with lamps. Recitations echo across the field of dharma.

Plan this journey →

61 days away

Makar Sankranti

Triveni Sangam · Prayagraj

The sun begins its northward journey. Pilgrims bathe at the confluence of three rivers — one of them invisible, all of them holy.

Plan this journey →

94 days away

Maha Shivratri

Kashi · The 12 Jyotirlingas

The longest night of devotion to Shiva. Temple bells from dusk to dawn. The Jyotirlinga circuit comes alive with rare ritual access.

Plan this journey →

128 days away

Holi at Barsana

Braj · Mathura-Vrindavan

Not the Holi the world knows. The original — Lathmar Holi, Phoolon ki Holi, ten days of Krishna's playful chaos in his own land.

Plan this journey →

186 days away

Char Dham Opens

Himalayas · Uttarakhand

The four sacred shrines emerge from winter. Akshaya Tritiya marks the opening. Six months of pilgrimage begin.
